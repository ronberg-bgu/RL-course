Question 1: Read the generated pddl/domain.pddl file. How does the (push-big) strict action constraint enforce that exactly two adjacent agents push the large box? What happens if three agents try to push it in the RL environment vs the PDDL domain?

Answer: 
In the PDDL domain, the `push-big` action enforces that exactly two agents push the large box by strictly defining its parameters to accept exactly two distinct agents (`?a1 ?a2 - agent`). The precondition mandates that these two agents must be located at `?from1` and `?from2`, which are strictly required to be adjacent to the two specific grid halves of the BigBox (`?boxloc1` and `?boxloc2`). Because the action signature binds exactly two agents and two locations behind the box, it is logically impossible for the PDDL planner to generate an action involving three agents.

In the PettingZoo RL environment, the physics engine translates this constraint geometrically. A BigBox occupies exactly 2 grid cells. Therefore, there are only 2 adjacent cells directly directly "behind" the box in any pushing direction. Since multiple agents cannot overlap on the exact same tile (`can_overlap() == False`), only a maximum of 2 agents can physically stand directly behind the BigBox to apply the necessary pushing vectors. If a 3rd agent tries to push from behind one of the two pushing agents, it will be blocked by the agent in front of it and its intent will not connect to the BigBox.
